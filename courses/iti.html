An **ITI (Industrial Training Institute)** course is a vocational training program for students who have completed Class 10. These courses are short-term and highly specialized, designed to give students specific job-oriented skills in a particular trade.

Think of it as learning a specific skill, like a craftsman's apprenticeship. If your child wants to get a job in a specific trade quickly, like a mechanic, electrician, or welder, an ITI course is the fastest way to get there. It's a direct path to a skilled labor job and a stable income.

Key Trades in ITI üî©

ITI programs are offered in many different trades. Some of the most common ones are:

Electrician: Learning how to install and maintain electrical wiring and systems.

Fitter: Working with machines and tools to assemble and repair mechanical parts.

Welder: Specializing in joining metal parts using welding techniques.

Motor Mechanic: Learning to repair and maintain engines and vehicles.

Plumber: Working with pipes and sanitation systems.

The training is very practical and focuses on the skills needed for a specific job, making graduates highly employable.

Career Paths After ITI üöÄ

An ITI certificate opens up several immediate career opportunities. Here are some of the most common career paths your child can pursue:

Direct Employment: ITI graduates are in high demand for entry-level positions in manufacturing companies, workshops, and construction sites. They can work as technicians, mechanics, or skilled laborers.

Apprenticeship: After completing the course, students can join an apprenticeship program with a company to get on-the-job training and a stipend. This often leads to a full-time job with the same company.

Self-Employment: With the practical skills they've gained, an ITI graduate can start their own business, such as an electrician's service, a small repair shop, or a plumbing service.

Further Education: Students can also continue their education by joining a polytechnic course through a lateral entry scheme.

An ITI course is a great choice if your child:

This path is a fast and effective way to gain a valuable skill and build a successful career.
