**MBBS (Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery)** is the most prestigious and demanding course for students who complete the BiPC (Biology, Physics, Chemistry) stream. It is a 5.5-year-long undergraduate medical degree that prepares students to become doctors.

Think of it as the path to becoming a healer. If your child has a deep-seated passion for helping people, is fascinated by the human body, and is ready for a long but incredibly rewarding journey, MBBS is the right choice. It is the **foundation** for a career in medicine and healthcare.

Key Subjects in MBBS üíâ

The MBBS curriculum is comprehensive, covering all aspects of medicine. Key subjects include:

Anatomy: The study of the structure of the human body.

Physiology: The study of how the human body functions.

Biochemistry: Understanding the chemical processes that occur within living organisms.

Pharmacology: The study of medicines and their effects on the body.

Pathology: The study of diseases and how they affect the body.

The course also includes a one-year internship where students get practical experience in a hospital setting.

Career Paths After MBBS ü©∫

An MBBS degree opens the door to a wide range of career opportunities in medicine. Here are some of the most common career paths your child can pursue:

General Practitioner: A general practitioner is a doctor who treats common illnesses and provides preventive care.

Specialization: After MBBS, doctors can pursue a postgraduate degree (MD or MS) to specialize in fields like Cardiology, Pediatrics, Surgery, or Dermatology.

Hospital Administration: Doctors can also work in hospital management and administration.

Medical Research: For those interested in research, a career in medical research involves studying diseases and developing new treatments.

Government Service: Doctors are highly sought after for positions in government hospitals and public health services.
