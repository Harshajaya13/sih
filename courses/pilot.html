A **Pilot** is a professional who operates an aircraft. Becoming a pilot is a unique career path that requires specialized training, a love for aviation, and a high level of responsibility. The path to becoming a commercial pilot often begins with a **Pilot's License** from a flying school.

Think of it as a career that takes you to the skies. If your child is fascinated by airplanes, is disciplined, and dreams of a career that is both adventurous and highly respected, this is a great choice. It is the **gateway** to a life of travel and adventure.

Steps to Become a Pilot üõ´

Becoming a pilot is a multi-step process. Here are the general steps:

Class 12: Students should have a background in Physics and Mathematics, as these subjects are crucial for understanding aviation principles.

Flying School: After Class 12, students must join a certified flying school to get their Commercial Pilot License (CPL). This involves a lot of flight training and theoretical exams.

Medical Fitness: Pilots must pass a very strict medical examination to ensure they are fit to fly.

Airline Training: After getting a CPL, aspiring pilots must go through training with a specific airline to learn to fly their aircraft.

The training is expensive and time-consuming but is the only way to become a professional pilot.

Career Paths for a Pilot üåê

A pilot's career is often straightforward but can lead to different opportunities. Here are some of the most common career paths your child can pursue:

Commercial Pilot: The most common path is to work for an airline, flying passenger or cargo planes.

Cargo Pilot: Pilots can also work for logistics companies, flying cargo planes all over the world.

Charter Pilot: A charter pilot flies smaller planes for private clients or businesses.

Flight Instructor: Experienced pilots can become flight instructors at a flying school.

Military Pilot: A different path is to join the air force as a military pilot. This requires a different selection process (like NDA).
