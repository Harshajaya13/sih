So, you're here. You want to build for the web, the most vast and accessible creative platform in human history. Your journey begins here, with HTML, the bedrock of every single website. Take a moment to appreciate that. You've just taken the most important step—the first one.

Right now, you might be looking at the source code of a webpage and seeing a confusing jumble of angle brackets and mysterious words. `

  • Prove You Are a Builder: We will show you that by learning just a handful of tags, you can create a real, structured document that can be viewed by anyone, anywhere in the world, on any device.

Your First Creative Sandbox: A Blank Text File

Your journey begins with the simplest tool imaginable: a blank text file. Name it `index.html`. That's it. That's your entire development environment. You will write in this file and open it directly in your web browser to see your creation come to life.

Why Is This the Right Place to Start?

HTML (HyperText Markup Language) is not a programming language; it's a *markup* language. Its job is to describe the *content* and give it *meaning*. A paragraph is a paragraph, a heading is a heading, a link is a link. By starting with a plain file, you see this relationship directly. You write `

Hello, World

`, save, refresh your browser, and you see "Hello, World" on the screen. It is the most direct and satisfying feedback loop in all of tech.

A common fear is, "My page will look ugly!" That's okay! HTML's job is not to make things beautiful; its job is to give them structure. Beauty comes later, with its partner, CSS. For now, we embrace the default styles of the browser. We are building the skeleton, not choosing the clothes.

Your Foundational Mission: A 3-Phase Plan to Confidence

Our mission is not to make you a "web developer." That comes later. Our mission is to make you *capable* and *confident* in creating a well-structured, meaningful web page. We will do this by focusing on the absolute essentials.
